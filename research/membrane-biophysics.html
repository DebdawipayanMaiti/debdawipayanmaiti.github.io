Cell Membrane Biophysics & Fluctuation Analysis

Summer Research Internship 2025 | Cell Biophysics Lab, IISER Kolkata

Research Motivation

This project aimed to understand how mechanical properties and thermal fluctuations govern cell membrane behavior, particularly during host–pathogen interactions.

Theoretical Modeling

Membranes were modeled using the Canham–Helfrich Hamiltonian. A harmonic approximation was used to study thermal fluctuations and extract parameters such as bending rigidity and surface tension. Protein–membrane interactions were incorporated into simulations.

Microscopy & Fluctuation Analysis

Phase-contrast microscopy was used to measure real-time membrane fluctuations in untreated interphase cells. Fluctuations were decomposed into spatial and temporal modes, and power spectral density maps were generated.

Imaging Resolution & Data Analysis

Epifluorescence microscopy was used to estimate imaging resolution via point spread function and FWHM analysis of fluorescent beads. Additional image analysis included kymographs and retraction speed calculations from live-cell TIFF stacks.

Scientific Outcome

This project strengthened my ability to connect theory with experiment and provided insight into how membrane mechanics regulate cellular response to external perturbations.
