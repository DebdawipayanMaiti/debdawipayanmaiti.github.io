Plant Mechanosensing & Movement Analysis

Course Project | IISER Kolkata

Scientific Motivation

The touch-sensitive plant Mimosa pudica exhibits rapid leaf-closing behavior. This project aimed to understand how mechanical stimuli are converted into electrical and mechanical responses at the whole-leaf level.

Experimental Setup

A custom cylindrical imaging setup was used to record time-lapse videos of leaflet motion following mechanical stimulation under varying hydration conditions.

Quantitative Kinematic Analysis

Leaflet angular displacement, velocity, and acceleration were extracted using image-based tracking. Box plots were generated to compare movement statistics across conditions.

Structural & Biophysical Interpretation

Pulvinus histology revealed differential deformation of extensor and flexor cells. Results supported an electro-mechanical model driven by turgor pressure changes and stimulus-dependent habituation.

Scientific Outcome

The study demonstrated that leaf movement is a rapid electro-mechanical process, linking cellular-scale signaling with macroscopic motion.
