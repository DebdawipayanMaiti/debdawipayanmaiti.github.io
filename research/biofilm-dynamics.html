Biofilm Dynamics & Single-Cell Motility

Independent Research Project | IISER Kolkata

Scientific Motivation

Bacterial biofilms represent an emergent multicellular state where individual motile cells collectively organize into spatially structured communities. This project aimed to understand how single-cell motility and physical constraints regulate collective biofilm growth.

Experimental Design

Two bacterial species (Vibrio and Azotobacter) were studied as monocultures and co-cultures. Parallel experiments were conducted in dilute liquid culture to analyze single-cell motion and on solid agar surfaces to quantify biofilm formation over time.

Single-Cell Motility Analysis

Bright-field microscopy videos were processed using MATLAB-based background subtraction and contrast enhancement. Individual bacteria were tracked for 15–20 seconds, and mean squared displacement (MSD) analysis was used to extract effective diffusion constants.

Biofilm Growth & Collective Motion

Fluorescence time-lapse imaging over three days enabled quantification of biofilm area expansion, boundary roughness, and fractal properties. Velocity fields extracted via Fourier-based convolution revealed vortex-like collective motion associated with enhanced biofilm growth.

Physical Interpretation

Differences between liquid and solid growth conditions were interpreted through changes in viscosity, surface tension, and resistive drag, highlighting the multiscale physical regulation of biofilm formation.

Skills & Concepts Developed

  • Single-particle tracking and MSD analysis
  • Velocity field estimation
  • Quantitative microscopy
  • Multiscale biophysical reasoning
