Research Experience

Biofilm Dynamics & Single-Cell Motility

Independent Project | IISER Kolkata

This project investigated how individual bacterial motion influences collective biofilm growth. Using Vibrio and Azotobacter monocultures and co-cultures, I combined bright-field and fluorescence microscopy with quantitative image analysis.

Conceptual illustration: From single-cell trajectories to collective biofilm expansion.

Mean squared displacement analysis was used to extract effective diffusion constants, while velocity fields derived from time-lapse imaging revealed vortex-like collective motion promoting enhanced biofilm growth.

Cell Membrane Biophysics & Fluctuation Analysis

Summer Research Intern 2025 | Cell Biophysics Lab, IISER Kolkata

During my summer internship, I studied biological membrane mechanics using the Canham–Helfrich Hamiltonian. By modeling membrane curvature elasticity and thermal fluctuations, I examined how bending rigidity and surface tension influence membrane deformation, particularly in the context of host–pathogen interactions.

In parallel, I performed phase-contrast microscopy on live interphase cells to quantify real-time membrane fluctuations. Spatial and temporal mode decomposition, power spectral density analysis, and fluctuation statistics were used to connect experimental observations with theoretical predictions. I also learned epifluorescence microscopy and basic confocal imaging, including point spread function and resolution analysis using fluorescent beads.

Plant Mechanosensing & Movement Analysis

Course Project | IISER Kolkata

This group project focused on the rapid leaf-closing response of the touch-sensitive plant Mimosa pudica. Using a custom-built imaging setup, we recorded time-lapse videos of leaflet closure under varying hydration conditions.

Image-based tracking enabled extraction of angular displacement, velocity, and acceleration of leaflets. Structural analysis of the pulvinus through histological staining supported an electro-mechanical model of movement driven by turgor pressure changes. The results demonstrated stimulus-dependent habituation and linked cellular-scale signaling to whole-leaf motion.
