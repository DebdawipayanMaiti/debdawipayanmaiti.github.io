About Me

I am a third-year BS–MS student in Biological Sciences at the Indian Institute of Science Education and Research (IISER) Kolkata. My academic journey has gradually converged toward biophysics, driven by a fascination with how collective biological phenomena emerge from simple physical interactions at the cellular scale.

Through independent and guided research projects, I have explored bacterial biofilm formation, cell membrane mechanics, and plant mechanosensing using a combination of microscopy, computational analysis, and physical modeling. I aspire to pursue a PhD in interdisciplinary biological physics, focusing on how mechanical forces and stochastic processes shape living systems.
